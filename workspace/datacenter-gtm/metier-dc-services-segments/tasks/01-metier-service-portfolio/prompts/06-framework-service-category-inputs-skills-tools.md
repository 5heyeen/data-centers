# Prompt 06: framework-service-category-inputs-skills-tools

> **Prompt:** For each service category, what inputs (skills, tools, frameworks, certifications) are required to deliver it credibly? What differentiates a tier-1 from a tier-3 provider in each category?

---

## Inputs and Tier Differentiation by Service Category

### 1. Strategy
**Inputs:** Structured frameworks (Porter's Five Forces, scenario planning, real options), financial modeling, stakeholder facilitation, industry benchmarking data, executive communication.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 brings sector-specific pattern recognition, proprietary benchmarking datasets, and practitioners who have held operational roles. Tier-3 applies generic frameworks without calibrating to the client's capital structure or competitive dynamics.

### 2. PMO / Programme Management
**Inputs:** PMI/PMBOK, PRINCE2, MSP, EVM, Primavera P6, MS Project, portfolio governance design, change management (Prosci/ADKAR).
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 embeds governance that changes behavior — escalation paths used, dashboards drive decisions, PMO outlasts the engagement. Tier-3 installs process artifacts (RAIDs, status reports) without ensuring adoption.

### 3. Owner's Engineer
**Inputs:** Civil, structural, MEP engineering credentials, technical due diligence methodology, constructability review, independent cost estimation, FEED/detailed design review, contract administration (NEC/FIDIC), commissioning protocols.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 has held the liability position — can challenge contractor claims with technical credibility. Tier-3 reviews documents but cannot push back authoritatively on specialist contractors.

### 4. Procurement & Contracts
**Inputs:** FIDIC/NEC/NS 8405-8407 (Norwegian standard contracts), TCO modeling, supplier qualification frameworks, negotiation methodology, e-procurement tools, category management.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 has run high-stakes competitive tenders and managed contractor defaults. Knows where contractual risk allocations fail in practice. Tier-3 follows procurement process templates without understanding the commercial leverage points.

### 5. Training & Competence Development
**Inputs:** Instructional design (ADDIE), adult learning theory, competency framework design, LMS platforms, facilitation skills, subject matter authority, assessment and certification design.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 builds training from live practitioner experience — real case studies, current practice. Tier-3 resells off-the-shelf content with light customization.

### 6. Digital / Technology Advisory
**Inputs:** TOGAF, data modeling, BIM (ISO 19650, Autodesk/Bentley), DCIM tools, IoT/OT integration awareness, ISO 27001, vendor-neutral evaluation methodology, change management for digital adoption.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 is genuinely vendor-neutral with implementation experience. Tier-3 has undisclosed vendor relationships or lacks experience operating tools under production conditions.

### 7. Risk Management
**Inputs:** ISO 31000, Monte Carlo simulation (@RISK/Palisade), risk register design, bowtie methodology, HAZID/HAZOP, insurance and contractual risk transfer knowledge.
**Tier 1 vs 3:** Tier-1 integrates risk into decision-making — drives contingency sizing and contract negotiation. Tier-3 produces risk registers that satisfy governance requirements but are disconnected from budget, schedule, and procurement decisions.
