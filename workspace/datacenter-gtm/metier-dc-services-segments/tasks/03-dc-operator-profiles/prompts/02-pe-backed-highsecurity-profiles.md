# Chunk 02: pe-backed-highsecurity-profiles
> **Topic:** PE-backed developer and high-security operator profiles
---

## 1. PE-Backed DC Developers (Finance-First Teams)

**Who they are.** PE-backed DC developers are founded or controlled by financial, investment, or real-estate professionals who identified the Norwegian hydropower cost advantage and raised institutional capital to build data centers before building an operations team. Norwegian examples include Fossefall (strategic investor-backed startup targeting 500 MW, SPV structure per site, CEO background in energy investment) and Kitebrook (principal-led vehicle backed by DigiPlex exit capital, 130 MW grid reserved, 1–4 employees in Norway). Bifrost Edge is a further target in this segment awaiting profile research.

**Team composition and capability gaps.** Leadership is typically one to three principals with finance, consulting, or energy backgrounds, supported by a legal adviser and a sales or commercial lead. What is consistently absent: project managers, cost controllers, planners, procurement/contracts specialists, and construction engineers. The ratio of ambition to execution staff is extreme — Fossefall announced 11 sites and 500 MW with no visible PM or EPC function; Kitebrook holds a ~NOK 1 billion pipeline with no internal engineering or procurement capability.

**How they buy advisory services.** Budget for advisory is typically unlocked at two trigger points: (1) when a tenant commitment or FID decision is imminent and the owner needs to demonstrate execution credibility to finalize the deal or capital raise, and (2) when investor due diligence requires a credible project plan with independent oversight. Before these triggers, these operators run very lean and resist committing to advisory retainers. The entry point is therefore pre-FID concept and procurement advisory, framed as "making your capital raise easier" rather than "managing your project."

**Investor-driven requirements.** Where external PE or infrastructure capital is involved — or being raised — investors impose requirements that directly create advisory demand: milestone-gated tranches require structured project controls; quarterly reporting obligations require dashboards and variance analysis the internal team cannot produce; board-level CAPEX approval thresholds require independent technical validation; ESG/EU Taxonomy mandates require a sustainability and PUE reporting framework. For operators like Fossefall seeking institutional capital in 2026, the act of engaging professional PM and Owner's Engineer advisory is itself a signal to investors that execution risk is being managed.

**Priority Metier services and entry points.**
- **Owner's Engineer / PMC** — highest-value entry for first-time developers. Frames Metier as the owner's technical representative, not a cost center.
- **Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning** — urgent before any EPC tender. At NOK 1 billion per site, wrong contract packaging is a catastrophic and irreversible mistake.
- **Project Execution & PM** — needed once construction is imminent. Best sold as capacity augmentation, not a sign of weakness.
- **Portfolio Governance** — activated when the operator moves from one site to multiple parallel builds. The shift to a portfolio model almost always catches finance-first teams unprepared.
- **Early-Phase Advisory** — relevant when a site is in feasibility. Concept optimization before CAPEX commitment is a low-cost engagement with high investor credibility value.

**Realistic entry point.** The most actionable opening is: "Before you go to tender, let us help you structure your contract packages — it costs far less to get it right now than to manage disputes during construction." This avoids the objection that "we're not ready for a PM yet" while still getting Metier into the project early, when influence is highest and lock-in is lowest.

## 2. High-Security Operators (Government, Defense, NATO Segment)

**Who they are.** High-security DC operators target government ministries, the Norwegian Armed Forces, NATO, NSM-classified entities, and critical national infrastructure clients. Skygard — a joint venture between Hafslund (energy), Telenor (telecom), and HitecVision (PE/energy), with NOK 2.4 billion committed to OSL1 in Oslo — is the primary Norwegian example. Lefdal Mine Datacenter in Vestland operates a sovereign cloud offering for sensitive public-sector workloads. This segment is small in operator count but large in project complexity and contract value.

**What makes procurement and construction uniquely complex.** High-security construction is not standard DC construction with extra access control. The entire procurement chain must be designed around the principle that anyone who touches classified infrastructure — from civil contractors to MEP subcontractors to equipment delivery crews — may require NSM security clearance (sikkerhetsklarering). Processing times for clearances run from weeks to many months and cannot be compressed. This has direct schedule implications: if a contractor or key subcontractor fails clearance or cannot obtain it, they must be replaced, potentially triggering re-tender. Contract packaging must anticipate this by separating cleared and non-cleared scopes, building clearance lead times into milestone schedules, and pre-qualifying a pool of cleared suppliers before tender is issued. Beyond clearance, NSM requirements may mandate: physical security zones (TEMPEST shielding, electromagnetic screening), specific approved equipment lists, restrictions on foreign-manufactured components, separation of IT fit-out from civil works by cleared personnel only, and audit logging of all site access during construction.

**Sikkerhetsklarering implications for contractor selection.** The pool of construction contractors and MEP firms with active security clearances in Norway is materially smaller than the general contractor market. This means: limited competitive tension in tendering (fewer bidders per package), higher unit prices (cleared contractors carry a premium), longer pre-qualification processes, and potential single-source situations for specialized components. The operator cannot simply run a standard open-competition EPC tender — the process must be designed from the outset around the constrained cleared-contractor pool. Procurement advisory for this segment is therefore fundamentally different from standard DC procurement: it requires knowledge of NSM clearance processes, experience qualifying contractors under security constraints, and the ability to structure contract packages that separate classified and non-classified scopes to maximize competition where it is legally permissible.

**Priority Metier services.**
- **Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning** — the most critical and most differentiated service for this segment. Structuring the procurement to navigate a constrained cleared-contractor market is a specialized capability that most DC procurement advisors do not have.
- **Project Execution & PM** — construction schedule management is highly complex when clearance timelines are a critical path item. A planner who does not understand sikkerhetsklarering will not build a realistic schedule.
- **Strategy Advisory** — relevant when an operator is defining which certifications to target (NSM COSMIC TOP SECRET vs. lower classifications), which client segments are addressable, and how to position against international high-security operators (e.g., UK government-certified DCs, Swedish MUST-approved facilities).
- **Portfolio Governance** — relevant for JV structures like Skygard where three institutional owners (Hafslund, Telenor, HitecVision) need unified reporting and structured stage-gate decisions.

**Where Metier can and cannot help.** Metier can help with: contract structure and procurement strategy, project management and schedule control, owner's engineer oversight of EPC contractors, governance frameworks for multi-owner JVs, and strategy advisory on segment positioning. Metier cannot help with: obtaining or sponsoring security clearances (clearances are granted to companies and individuals by NSM, not by advisors), advising on classified technical specifications or data handling, or providing cleared project managers who can access classified areas — unless those individuals hold personal clearances. This is the key boundary: Metier's role is to help the operator design a procurement and delivery system that accommodates security constraints, not to hold or manage the clearances themselves. If Metier is to serve this segment at scale, building a cleared PM/engineering cadre should be evaluated as a medium-term strategic option.

**Realistic entry point.** The most actionable opening for Skygard or comparable operators is: "Your security requirements fundamentally change your contract strategy — the cleared-contractor pool is small, clearance timelines are a critical path item, and standard EPC models do not work. We can help you design a procurement framework built around these constraints before you go to tender." This is credible, specific to their actual problem, and positions Metier as a specialist rather than a generalist advisor.
