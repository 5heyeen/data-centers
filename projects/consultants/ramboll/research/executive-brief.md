# Ramboll DC Competitive Intelligence — Executive Brief

**Prepared:** 2026-04-17 | **For:** Metier DC GTM Strategy

---

## TL;DR

Ramboll is a Danish-headquartered global engineering consultancy building a **top-3 global data center consulting practice** through two acquisitions: **EYP Mission Critical Facilities (Jan 2022)** — a US mission-critical design specialist — and **i3 Solutions Group (Sept 2024)** — a UK/Singapore sustainability consultancy. Their combined practice has **300+ dedicated DC experts globally** and positions on **sustainability + mission-critical engineering depth**.

**Critical insight for Metier:** Ramboll does NOT offer owner's representative or project management services for data centers. They are an engineering partner, not a PM competitor. This means **Metier and Ramboll occupy complementary, not overlapping, roles.**

---

## What Ramboll Sells (Data Center Services)

1. **Strategic IT Advisory** — Hybrid IT and Adaptive Stack framework, Sourcing and Demand Analysis methodology (EYP MCF's upstream offering)
2. **Full-stack Engineering Design** — Architectural, structural, MEP, cooling, power, security, fire (all disciplines)
3. **Mission-Critical Expertise** — Tier III/IV certified designs; claimed first Tier IV facilities in US, Saudi Arabia, China
4. **Modular/Prefab Delivery** — Documented at scale (Dallas 700K sq ft colo, 45 EcoPODs)
5. **Commissioning** — Integrated with design from concept stage
6. **Operations & Risk** — Probability Risk Analysis (PRA) for existing infrastructure
7. **Sustainability & Net-Zero** — Published September 2025 roadmap; waste heat + district heating integration
8. **Industry Metric Contribution** — Co-developed IUE (extends PUE) via The Green Grid/TGGC

**Notable absence:** No explicit owner's rep, program management, or cost consultancy services for data centers.

---

## The EYP "Playbook" — What It Actually Is

**Clarification:** "EYP" in this context = **EYP Mission Critical Facilities (EYP MCF)** — a specialist data center consultancy, NOT EYP Architecture+Engineering broadly. They share historical roots but are different entities.

**No single "playbook" document exists.** The term refers to their integrated delivery approach, which combines:

- **Strategic advisory** (Hybrid IT/Adaptive Stack)
- **Multi-year sourcing methodology** (Sourcing and Demand Analysis)
- **Risk assessment methodology** (PRA)
- **End-to-end engineering** (strategy → commissioning)
- **Modular delivery capability**
- **Mission-critical specialization** (25+ years, 70M sq ft claimed)

**Key heritage projects:** NCAR-Wyoming Supercomputing Center (award-winning), NCSA Petascale Computing Building, HP/HPE enterprise DCs (during 2007-2018 subsidiary era).

**Most recent major win:** Texas Critical Data Centers 438-acre AI campus (announced Oct 2025, initial 400 MW, potential 1 GW).

## Norway Activity — The Critical Gap

**Finding:** Despite 14 Norwegian offices and ~1,600 Norwegian staff, **no Norwegian data center project publicly credits Ramboll as the lead engineering consultant.**

**Norwegian operator landscape (all with NO public Ramboll credit):**
- Green Mountain (Stavanger, Rjukan, Oslo sites)
- STACK Infrastructure / DigiPlex (OSL01 Ulven)
- Bulk Infrastructure (N01 Kristiansand — 600 MW campus)
- Lefdal Mine Datacenter (ABB and Rittal are credited instead)
- Google Skien hyperscale DC (EUR 600M, operational 2026) — no known Ramboll role

**Likely explanations** (in order of probability):
1. Commercial confidentiality (most likely)
2. Sub-consultant roles not publicly attributed
3. Genuine gap in Norwegian DC track record

## Nordic References Ramboll Does Have

1. **Meta (Facebook) Odense, Denmark** — Ramboll-designed energy centre redistributing DC waste heat to Odense's district heating. **Largest Danish heat pump using DC surplus heat.** Marquee Nordic sustainability reference.
2. **TeliaSonera Helsinki, Finland (2018)** — Full-lifecycle engagement (feasibility through handover). 24 MW, LEED Gold, supplies heat to 9,000 households.

These are strong **Nordic sustainability credentials** — but **not Norwegian credentials**.

## Strategic Implications for Metier

### Positioning
**Do not compete with Ramboll on engineering depth.** Position as **owner-side PM advisor** — a complementary, not competing, role.

### Metier's defensible advantages
1. Norwegian PM methodology and project culture fluency
2. Owner-side role independence (no design-PM conflict of interest)
3. Norwegian operator relationships (if established)
4. Working-level Norwegian language and regulatory fluency

### Where Ramboll is a real threat
1. **Strategic DC advisory mandates** where a client wants one firm for strategy → design → commissioning (EYP MCF's Hybrid IT framework)
2. **Sustainability-led advisory** where a client wants combined sustainability + engineering scope (Ramboll net-zero roadmap + i3/EYP engineering)

### Partnership plays
- **Waste heat to district heating** projects in Norway — Ramboll as engineer, Metier as PM
- **Hyperscaler campuses** in Norway — Metier as owner's PM, Ramboll or competitor as engineer
- **AI infrastructure** — Metier as local PM, Ramboll as AI/high-density design specialist
