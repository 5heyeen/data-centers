## Supplementary Sources to Fill Website Blind Spots

Building on the blind spots identified (prompt 13) and the website analysis methodology (prompt 12), the following sources fill the gaps most effectively:

**1. Job postings (highest signal-to-noise ratio)**
LinkedIn, finn.no, and the firm's own careers page reveal what the firm is actively building. Roles advertised — and how they are titled — reveal which service lines are growing vs. static. Specialist role titles (e.g., "Senior Cost Controller," "Commissioning Manager," "Owner's Engineer") indicate core operational capability. Generic titles ("Project Manager," "Consultant") indicate staffing scale, not depth. Scan 12–24 months of postings to identify trends.

**2. LinkedIn — employee profiles at scale**
Search for current and former employees, filter by role keywords. The distribution of specialist titles across the firm reveals where real depth sits. Former employees' career histories show which sectors and service lines people came from — indicating the talent pipeline the firm draws on. Alumni who moved to DC operators or contractors are a strong signal of sector adjacency.

**3. Conference presentations and publications**
Presentations at industry conferences (PMI, ICPM, DC-specific events like DCD or Datacloud) reveal where the firm's thought leaders are investing intellectual effort. Published articles, white papers, and webinar appearances indicate which service lines have enough depth to generate genuine insights — not just marketing copy.

**4. Financial filings and Brønnøysundregisteret (Norway)**
Norwegian AS companies file annual accounts. Revenue trends, headcount, and equity changes reveal firm health and growth trajectory. For Metier: cross-reference reported revenue with stated headcount to assess average fee rates and implied service mix.

**5. Internal documents (when accessible)**
Proposal documents, methodology guides, and framework descriptions shared by former clients or in public procurement portals give the deepest view of actual delivery methodology. These are rare but highly valuable.

**6. Reference calls**
The single most reliable source. Former clients will describe what the firm actually did, not what it claims. For DC applicability assessment: specifically ask whether the engagement was led by sector-specialist staff or generalist project managers who learned on the job.

**Priority sequence for Metier assessment:** Job postings → LinkedIn employee profiles → metier.no website → published content → financial filings → reference calls (if available).
