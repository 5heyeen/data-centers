### Hiring Pattern Summary

Fossefall's hiring pattern is characteristic of a **very early-stage infrastructure developer** that is building foundational corporate functions before scaling into project execution:

1. **Current hiring is corporate/support, not project delivery.** The two active finn.no ads (People & Talent Specialist, Legal Counsel) are about building internal organizational infrastructure — HR processes and legal capacity — not about delivering construction projects.

2. **Extremely lean team for the stated ambition.** With only 6 registered employees and a stated target of 500 MW across 11+ Nordic sites with first financial close in 2026, Fossefall has an enormous gap between ambition and visible organizational capacity.

3. **Finance-heavy founding team.** The CEO and CFO both come from investment/finance backgrounds. The recently hired VP Construction is the first visible technical/delivery hire. This confirms the seed context that founders are from finance backgrounds — the company is being built as an investment platform first, with delivery capability to follow.

4. **No public postings for Engineering Manager.** The seed context mentions this role is open, but it was not found on finn.no or LinkedIn. This suggests Fossefall may be using headhunters or personal networks for senior technical hires, which is common for small startups in the Norwegian DC market.

5. **Hiring a People & Talent Specialist signals rapid scaling intent.** They are building the recruitment function itself, indicating they expect a significant hiring wave and want internal capacity to manage it rather than relying entirely on external recruiters.

### Capability Gaps Inferred

Given Fossefall's stated plans (500 MW, 11 sites, first financial close 2026, Fyresdal acquisition), the following critical capabilities appear **absent or not yet being recruited for**:

| Expected Capability | Evidence of Gap |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| **Project management / project controls** | No PM, cost controller, planner, or scheduler roles posted. For a company approaching financial close on its first major project, this is a significant gap. |
| **Procurement & contracts** | No procurement manager, contract manager, or supply chain roles visible. A 500 MW program will require major equipment procurement (transformers, generators, cooling, UPS) and EPC/EPCM contractor selection. |
| **Construction management** | VP Construction recently hired, but no construction managers, site managers, or field engineers. One VP cannot deliver multi-site construction alone. |
| **Technical engineering** | Engineering Manager open but not posted publicly. No electrical, mechanical, or civil engineering roles visible. |
| **Operations & facilities** | No data center operations roles (despite acquiring Fyresdal Datacenter, which presumably needs operations staff). |
| **IT / DCIM / networking** | No roles visible in this category. |
| **ESG / sustainability** | No dedicated sustainability or ESG roles, despite the company's core narrative being built around clean energy and renewable-powered AI. |

**In summary:** Fossefall is currently a finance-and-deal-making organization with 6 people, building its corporate skeleton (HR, legal). The entire project delivery apparatus — PM, procurement, engineering, construction management, operations — is either absent or being sourced in ways not visible to public job searches.

### Implications for Metier

Fossefall's hiring pattern presents a **strong and multi-dimensional opportunity** for Metier. The gap between stated ambition (500 MW, 11 sites, financial close 2026) and visible delivery capability (6 employees, no PM/procurement/construction team) is one of the largest we are likely to see among Norwegian DC operators.

**Priority Metier services (highest relevance):**

1. **Project Execution & Project Management (Service 2)** — CRITICAL. Fossefall has a VP Construction but zero visible project management, cost control, or planning capability. With financial close targeted for 2026, they will need project directors, PMs, cost controllers, and planners almost immediately. Metier can provide the entire PM backbone while Fossefall focuses on deals and capital.

2. **Owner's Engineer / PMC (Service 4)** — VERY HIGH. Fossefall is a financial-DNA company entering physical infrastructure delivery. They fit the archetype perfectly: PE/financial investors without construction expertise, wanting a lean internal team but needing technical oversight of contractors and suppliers.

3. **Contract, Procurement & Equipment Planning (Service 1)** — VERY HIGH. No procurement function visible. A 500 MW program requires sophisticated contract packaging (EPC vs. EPCM strategy), long-lead equipment procurement, and supplier qualification. This needs to start well before construction begins.

4. **Early-Phase Advisory (Service 3)** — HIGH. With 11 sites under evaluation and first financial close approaching, Fossefall needs concept selection, PDRI, and front-end planning support. Their investors will expect rigorous decision-making frameworks to justify capital deployment.

5. **Portfolio & Project Owner Governance (Service 5)** — HIGH. Multiple sites across Norway and Sweden, institutional investors, and rapid scaling all point to a need for portfolio governance frameworks and structured reporting — especially important for presenting to investors.

6. **Strategy & Business Development Advisory (Service 6)** — MODERATE. As a new market entrant with broad ambitions, Fossefall may benefit from organizational design support to match their growth trajectory. The hire of a People & Talent Specialist suggests they are thinking about this.

7. **Competence Development & Training (Service 7)** — MODERATE (future). As Fossefall scales its team, structured onboarding and PM capability building will become relevant, but this is a later-phase need.

**Key timing insight:** Fossefall is approaching financial close on its first projects in 2026. The window for Metier to engage is **now** — once financial close happens, the need for PM, procurement, and owner's engineer services becomes immediate and urgent. A proactive approach before financial close positions Metier as a strategic partner rather than a reactive hire.
