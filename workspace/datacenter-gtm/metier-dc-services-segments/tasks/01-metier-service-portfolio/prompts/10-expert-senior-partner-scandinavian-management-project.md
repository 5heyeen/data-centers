# Prompt 10: expert-senior-partner-scandinavian-management-project

> **Prompt:** You are a senior partner at a Scandinavian management and project consultancy assessing your own firm's service portfolio. What questions would you ask before declaring the portfolio complete and coherent?

---

## Portfolio Completeness & Coherence Review: Questions a Rigorous Partner Would Ask

### 1. Delivery Capability
- Do we have demonstrable, repeatable delivery methodology for each service, or are some offerings dependent on one or two key individuals who cannot be scaled?
- Where is our bench strength genuinely deep versus where are we one resignation away from a capability gap?
- For each service, can we define what "good" looks like in delivery terms — measurable outputs, quality gates, and client-facing milestones?
- Which services require specialist subcontractors, and have we stress-tested those dependencies under capacity pressure?

### 2. Market Fit
- For each service, can we name five specific client archetypes who have a recurring, budget-backed need?
- Are our services calibrated to the decision-making cycles of our target clients?
- Which services are genuinely differentiated for the Norwegian/Scandinavian market context — versus generic offerings any international firm could replicate?
- Are there adjacent pain points our clients consistently raise that we are currently not addressing?

### 3. Competitive Positioning
- For each service, who is the primary competitor — a large international firm, a boutique specialist, or the client's own internal capability — and do we have a credible answer to why we win?
- Are we positioned on price, quality, speed, or sector expertise? Is that positioning consistent across the portfolio?
- Where do we win deals, and where do we lose them? Have we audited loss reasons systematically?
- Are any services in the portfolio primarily defensive (retained to avoid losing clients) rather than genuinely profitable?

### 4. Internal Coherence
- Can a client who buys Service A be naturally led toward Services B and C — is there a logical client journey?
- Does the portfolio create or exploit any shared intellectual property, frameworks, or data assets across service lines?
- Are margins and utilization targets consistent, or are some services systematically subsidizing others?
- When we bring multiple service lines to a single client, do they reinforce each other's credibility or create confusion?

**A portfolio passes this review only when every service has a named buyer archetype, a clear competitor, a scalable delivery model, and a logical connection to at least one adjacent service.**
