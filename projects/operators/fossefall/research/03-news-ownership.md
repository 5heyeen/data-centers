### Funding Situation

**Total seed capital raised:** ~NOK 60 million

**Funding stage:** Seed / pre-institutional. No reported PE or VC rounds beyond the angel/HNW investors listed above.

**Future capital needs:** Fossefall plans to raise "several billion NOK" over the next two years to fund its 500 MW development pipeline. Individual sites alone are estimated at NOK 1–3 billion each. First projects targeting financial close in 2026.

*[Inference]* The NOK 60M seed is modest relative to the 500 MW ambition. The company will need to raise significantly more — likely through project finance, infrastructure funds, or strategic partnerships — to execute its pipeline. The SPV structure facilitates site-by-site financing with different investor pools per project.

#### Investor Requirements & Constraints

- **Return targets:** No PE-style hard IRR gates. Current investors are angels, family offices, and strategic corporates — more flexible than institutional capital. However, the planned multi-billion NOK raise will likely bring institutional investors with 8-15% IRR expectations.
- **Exit horizon:** No fixed exit timeline from current investors. Seekr's 36-month capacity agreement creates a near-term delivery milestone (operational AI capacity by ~end 2026). The future institutional raise will introduce exit pressure.
- **Milestone gates:** Seekr's investment is effectively milestone-gated: Fossefall must deliver operational capacity for SeekrFlow deployment. Future project finance will be formally milestone-gated (land → permitting → grid → construction → commissioning).
- **ESG mandates:** Valinor (Helvig) has a strong clean-energy thesis — Fossefall must maintain the renewable energy narrative. Future institutional investors (pension funds, SFDR-classified funds) will require EU Taxonomy alignment, PUE reporting, and renewable energy certification.
- **Reporting burden:** Light from current angel investors. Will increase dramatically with institutional capital — quarterly financials, construction progress dashboards, KPI reporting across SPVs.
- **Governance provisions:** Current governance appears lean (Eirik Wiik as board chair). Seekr at 15.6% likely has a board seat or observer rights. Future institutional investors will demand board seats, CAPEX approval thresholds, reserved matters, and structured reporting.

#### Capital Deployment Status

- **Committed:** ~NOK 60M seed capital
- **Deployed:** Fyresdal acquisition (price undisclosed), early development costs across pipeline
- **Remaining need:** Multi-billion NOK. Each major site (Harpefoss ~NOK 3B, Rollag >NOK 1B) requires orders of magnitude more than current seed
- **Deployment timeline pressure:** First financial close targeted 2026. Seekr's 36-month capacity reservation creates concrete delivery deadline. The gap between committed capital (~NOK 60M) and required capital (billions) is the single biggest risk factor.

**Key risk for next raise:** Current investor base is predominantly angels and family offices. Institutional infrastructure investors will demand: project-level SPVs (✓ already in place), contracted revenue (Seekr agreement helps), permitting certainty (not yet achieved for most sites), and bankable PPAs. The current investor base validates the story but does not provide the governance architecture that institutional capital will require. **This is a direct Metier opportunity — Portfolio Governance and structured project controls make Fossefall bankable.**

### Ownership & Funding Implications

1. **No PE exit pressure today — but it's coming.** Current investors are patient, but the multi-billion raise will introduce institutional capital with stricter requirements. Fossefall has a window to put governance and project controls in place before institutional due diligence begins.

2. **Seekr is the most demanding investor now.** The 15.6% stake + 36-month capacity reservation creates hard delivery milestones. Fossefall must have operational AI capacity at Fyresdal by end 2026. Failure to deliver would damage the most strategically important investor relationship.

3. **The SPV model is smart but multiplies complexity.** Each site needs its own business case, permitting, financing, contractor management, and investor reporting. With 11+ sites and 6-7 employees, this is unsustainable without external PM support.

4. **Valinor/Helvig connection provides energy network but not construction capability.** Helvig's portfolio is renewable energy (wind, solar, batteries) — adjacent to DC but not the same. The relationship provides credibility and network in power procurement, but not in DC construction delivery.

5. **Strategic investors double as customers — creates alignment but dependency.** Seekr (capacity reservation) and Done.ai (GPUaaS) are both customers and investors. This de-risks revenue but concentrates customer risk. Institutional investors will want to see customer diversification.

### Regulatory & Permitting Status

**Fyresdal (Telemark) — Lowest risk.** Acquired facility already holds reserved grid capacity. No new grid concession needed for initial 5.5 MW. CBRE greenfield expansion will require additional permitting. No public opposition reported. Operations targeted for 2026.

**Harpefoss, Sør-Fron (Innlandet) — Significant local opposition.** Site (closed school) is zoned for commercial use ("næringsformål"), but strong public backlash erupted Feb 2026: concerns over siting DC in cultural landscape, noise, power consumption, and Seekr's US military ties. Mayor postponed option agreement signing 10 Feb; public meeting held 11 March. Outcome not yet confirmed. Grid connection requires Statnett queue.

**Rollag, Buskerud (Numedal) — Early stage, emerging debate.** Fossefall plans to acquire old Kongsberg Vapenfabrikk factory for 15 MW facility. Local debate over ~130 GWh/yr power consumption and few jobs. Critics call for pausing DC plans until national regulatory framework is finalized. No formal permitting application yet.

**New Norwegian DC regulations affecting Fossefall:**
1. **Datasenterforskriften (Jan 2025):** All DC operators must register with Nkom, disclosing ownership, services, and power consumption.
2. **New zoning rules (Jul 2025):** "Datasenter" is now a distinct land-use category. General commercial/industrial zoning is no longer sufficient — DCs must be explicitly designated. This could complicate Harpefoss and Rollag.
3. **Concession debate (Stortinget, ongoing):** Broad parliamentary majority has asked government to assess whether a national DC concession system (via NVE) should be introduced. Would require national-level approval per site.
4. **Spillvarme (waste heat) requirements:** Stortinget has called for mandatory waste-heat utilization above certain capacity thresholds, affecting 15-30 MW sites.

### Key Takeaways

1. **Fossefall's current investor base is patient but lightweight.** Angels and family offices provide flexibility but not the governance infrastructure that institutional capital will demand. The planned multi-billion raise is the critical inflection point — Fossefall must demonstrate execution credibility before institutional due diligence.

2. **Seekr's 36-month capacity reservation is the hardest near-term milestone.** Operational AI capacity at Fyresdal by end 2026 is non-negotiable for maintaining the most important investor/customer relationship. This is where Metier's Project Execution & PM services are most immediately relevant.

3. **The SPV model + 11 sites + 6 employees = unsustainable without external PM.** Each SPV needs project controls, cost management, contractor oversight, and investor reporting. This is a direct fit for Metier's Portfolio Governance and Owner's Engineer services.

4. **Regulatory risk is increasing.** New zoning rules, potential concession requirements, and waste-heat mandates add complexity to every site. Fossefall needs regulatory/permitting expertise across its portfolio — another Metier touchpoint.

5. **The Valinor/Helvig connection validates the clean-energy thesis but doesn't solve execution.** Fossefall's energy network is strong; its construction delivery capability is the gap. Metier fills exactly this gap.
