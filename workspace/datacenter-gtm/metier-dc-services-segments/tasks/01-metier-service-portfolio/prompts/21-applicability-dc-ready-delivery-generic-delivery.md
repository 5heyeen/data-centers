# Prompt 21: applicability-dc-ready-delivery-generic-delivery

> **Prompt:** For each service category identified, what would "DC-ready delivery" look like vs. a generic delivery of the same service? What adaptations are needed?

---

## DC-Ready vs. Generic Delivery by Service Category

### 1. Project Management / PMO
**Generic delivery:** Stage-gate framework, weekly status reports, RAID log, escalation matrix. Milestone tracking against a baselined schedule.
**DC-ready delivery:** Integrated MEP schedule with system-level interdependencies modelled; compressed decision windows aligned to hyperscaler procurement cycles; investor-grade reporting dashboards (IRR impact of schedule variance, milestone-gated capital release); power and cooling critical path explicitly tracked; parallel workstream management across civil, electrical, mechanical, and IT fit-out with formal interface management.

### 2. Owner's Engineer / Owner's Representative
**Generic delivery:** Review contractor submittals, attend site meetings, manage punch list, coordinate handover.
**DC-ready delivery:** Independent review of N+1/2N redundancy configurations; validation of long-lead equipment specifications (generators, UPS, transformers, chillers) against design intent; commissioning protocol review including Level 1–5 testing sequences; security clearance management for contractor access (for high-security operators); interface management between base-build contractor and IT fit-out contractor.

### 3. Procurement & Contract Management
**Generic delivery:** Develop tender documents, run RFP, evaluate bids, award contract, administer post-award.
**DC-ready delivery:** Front-load procurement of long-lead equipment (40–80 week lead times for transformers and generators require procurement decisions at FEED stage, not after detailed design); develop secure supply chain protocols for cleared contractors (high-security segment); structure EPC vs EPCM vs CM decision based on operator risk appetite and timeline; include power purchase agreement and grid connection procurement in scope.

### 4. Risk Management
**Generic delivery:** Risk register, qualitative heat map, Monte Carlo schedule/cost model, contingency recommendation.
**DC-ready delivery:** Risk library populated with DC-specific risks (power supply failure during commissioning, cooling system design error, fiber route conflict, cybersecurity exposure during IT fit-out, regulatory delay on grid connection consent); scenario analysis for power redundancy failures during ramp-up; insurance gap analysis specific to DC operational risks.

### 5. Training & Competence Development
**Generic delivery:** PRINCE2/PMP certification, PM methodology workshops, leadership development.
**DC-ready delivery:** DC project management fundamentals (MEP interfaces, commissioning sequencing, Tier classification implications for build strategy); procurement training specific to DC equipment categories; security clearance management awareness training (for high-security operators).

### 6. Strategy & Advisory
**Generic delivery:** Market sizing, competitive landscape, strategic options assessment, business case.
**DC-ready delivery:** DC market positioning (hyperscale vs. colo vs. high-security vs. edge); power cost geography analysis; latency topology mapping; EU Taxonomy and PUE compliance strategy for investor mandates; operator archetype selection (build-to-suit vs. speculative vs. campus); capital structure advice aligned to DC asset lifecycle (20+ year asset with 3–7 year PE hold).

### 7. Portfolio Governance
**Generic delivery:** Portfolio dashboard, stage-gate framework, resource allocation model, board reporting.
**DC-ready delivery:** Multi-site MW tracking against investor commitments; milestone-gated capital release structure aligned to debt covenants and equity tranches; standardised reporting across sites for institutional investor quarterly reporting; ESG and PUE metric integration into portfolio dashboard.
