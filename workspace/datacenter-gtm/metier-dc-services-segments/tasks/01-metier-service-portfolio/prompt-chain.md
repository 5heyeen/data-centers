# Socratic Research Prompts: Metier Service Portfolio Catalogue

> **Topic:** Document Metier's complete service offering from public website + internal docs to establish the full capability picture for DC applicability assessment.
> **Use these prompts in sequence in any AI tool (ChatGPT, Claude, etc.) for deep, structured research.**

---

## Principles

- `[independent]` What makes a professional services firm's service catalogue genuinely comprehensive vs. superficially broad? What distinguishes firms that can truly execute across many service lines from those that list services they can't deliver?
- `[independent]` How do management/project consultancies typically organise their service lines — by industry, capability, engagement type, or client need? What are the trade-offs of each approach?
- `[independent]` What is the difference between a service line a firm markets vs. a capability it actually delivers at scale? How can you tell from public information whether a listed service is core or peripheral?
- `[independent]` When assessing a consultancy's portfolio for a new vertical (like data centers), what signals indicate genuine transferability of services vs. superficial relabelling?

---

## Framework

- `[independent]` What are the main categories of services a project management and strategy consultancy typically offers? List the standard taxonomy (e.g., strategy, PMO, owner's engineer, training, digital, procurement).
- `[independent]` For each service category, what inputs (skills, tools, frameworks, certifications) are required to deliver it credibly? What differentiates a tier-1 from a tier-3 provider in each category?
- `[sequential]` Given the categories above, how would you map them to the project lifecycle — from concept/feasibility through to operations? Which services cover early-phase, execution-phase, and close-out?
- `[independent]` What certifications, accreditations, or frameworks (PRINCE2, PMI, ISO, etc.) are most valued by clients buying project management services? How do these signal capability?
- `[sequential]` Building on the lifecycle and certification framework: how should a consultancy communicate its portfolio to maximise relevance to capital project owners? What language and framing resonates?

---

## Expert Simulation

- `[independent]` You are a senior partner at a Scandinavian management and project consultancy assessing your own firm's service portfolio. What questions would you ask before declaring the portfolio complete and coherent?
- `[independent]` What information on a consultancy's website most reliably indicates the depth of their capability — case studies, team bios, accreditations, client lists, or something else?
- `[sequential]` Given the signals above, how would you analyse a consultancy website to produce a reliable service catalogue that reflects real capability, not just marketing copy?
- `[independent]` What parts of a consultancy's portfolio are typically under-represented on their public website? Why might strong capabilities be invisible or poorly communicated externally?
- `[sequential]` With those blind spots in mind, what supplementary sources (job ads, LinkedIn, case studies, internal documents) would you use to fill gaps in the public picture?

---

## Failure Analysis

- `[independent]` What are the most common mistakes when assessing a consultancy's service portfolio from public sources only? What false conclusions do people reach?
- `[independent]` How does a professional services firm's website often misrepresent its actual focus — overselling breadth, underselling depth, or vice versa?
- `[sequential]` Given these common misrepresentations, what validation steps would you take to sense-check a portfolio catalogue produced from website content alone?
- `[independent]` When firms enter new verticals (like data centers), what services do they mistakenly lead with vs. what actually gets traction? Why?

---

## Applicability Assessment

- `[independent]` What criteria should be used to assess whether a general-purpose project management service is genuinely applicable to the data center sector specifically? Consider: technical complexity, regulatory environment, client types, procurement norms.
- `[independent]` Which professional services categories have the highest natural transferability to data center construction and operations, and which require the most adaptation?
- `[sequential]` For each service category identified, what would "DC-ready delivery" look like vs. a generic delivery of the same service? What adaptations are needed?
