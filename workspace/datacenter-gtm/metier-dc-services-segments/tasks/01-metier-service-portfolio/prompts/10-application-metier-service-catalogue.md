## Part 5: Peripheral Services Flagged

**1. Digital Initiatives (Service 8):** Described in internal docs as "less directly relevant." The DC market has strong specialist competition for DCIM and BIM. Risk: leading with digital advisory in DC conversations signals misunderstanding of the sector.

**2. Competence Development as an entry service:** Strong capability (16,000 participants/year), but DC operators don't call a training provider when they need project help. Better as a follow-on once PMO/OE relationships are established.

**3. Generic "strategy advisory" without DC credentials:** Without published DC references, strategy advisory claims lack credibility. Operators expect sector depth, not generic strategic frameworks.

---

## Conclusions

1. **Metier has a strong foundation for DC advisory** — 6 of their 8 internal DC services have high transferability and are needed by DC operators today.
2. **The public website creates an invisibility problem** — no DC framing means no inbound discovery. GTM requires direct outreach, not SEO.
3. **Three services are natural entry points:** Owner's Engineer, Project Execution & PM, and Contract & Procurement — all scoped, high-stakes, near-term problems that don't require pre-existing trust.
4. **Three services are strong follow-ons:** Portfolio Governance, Early-Phase Advisory, and Competence Development — these deepen the relationship once in the door.
5. **Metier needs engineering partners** for any service requiring DC-specific design (cooling topology, power architecture, fire engineering) — this is a structural gap that won't be solved by training.
