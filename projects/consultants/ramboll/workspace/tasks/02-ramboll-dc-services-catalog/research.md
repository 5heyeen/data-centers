## Executive Summary

Ramboll's data center services — delivered across three brands (Ramboll base + EYP Mission Critical Facilities + i3 Solutions Group) — cover the **full project lifecycle from strategic advisory through operations**, with particular depth in:
1. Mission-critical engineering design (EYP MCF heritage)
2. Strategic IT advisory (EYP MCF — Hybrid IT / Adaptive Stack framework)
3. Sustainability and net-zero data center design (i3 Solutions + Ramboll environmental practice)
4. Commissioning and operational risk assessment (EYP MCF)

**Notable absence:** No explicit "owner's representative" or "program management" service line for data centers has been identified in public materials. Ramboll's DC positioning is primarily engineering/design advisory, not owner-side PM.

## Section 7: Key Takeaways for Metier

1. **Ramboll/EYP MCF/i3 has a deep engineering design catalog but NOT a project management catalog.** This confirms the competitive positioning: Ramboll is an engineering partner, Metier is a project management partner. They operate in adjacent, complementary segments.

2. **The overlap zones to watch are strategic IT advisory and sustainability advisory** — these are where an enterprise client might choose EYP MCF's Hybrid IT framework over a Metier-led strategic PM engagement, or where an operator might prefer i3/Ramboll's net-zero roadmap framework over a Metier sustainability PM approach.

3. **For Norwegian/Nordic hyperscale and colo mandates**, Metier's local PM expertise + Ramboll's engineering design capability could be a **complementary partnership** rather than competitors. The i3 acquisition (2024) indicates Ramboll is expanding in Europe but is still establishing Nordic DC references.

4. **Ramboll's waste heat recovery / district heating expertise** (via their Nordic building/energy practice) is a strong local capability that Metier should recognize as both a competitor's asset and a potential partner capability.
