# Prompt 16: failure-website-misrepresent-actual-focus

> **Prompt:** How does a professional services firm's website often misrepresent its actual focus — overselling breadth, underselling depth, or vice versa?

---

## How Consultancy Websites Misrepresent Actual Focus

**The dominant distortion: overselling breadth, underselling depth.**

This is structurally rational from a BD perspective. Breadth protects against disqualification — if the website signals deep specialisation in oil and gas PM, the firm risks being screened out of data center, renewables, or public sector opportunities before a conversation starts. Generic language ("complex capital programmes," "major infrastructure projects") keeps more doors open. The cost: genuine depth in any one area becomes invisible.

This creates a specific failure mode for buyers: the firm's website looks identical to a dozen competitors. Differentiation only happens in conversation, through CV review, and reference checks — stages many procurement processes never reach.

**Second distortion: temporal misalignment.** The website reflects the firm's founding thesis or a past strategic pivot, not its current capability mix. A firm that built its reputation in public sector PM but has spent five years on private infrastructure finance still shows public sector imagery and language.

**Third distortion (boutiques): overselling depth, underselling breadth.** A firm that has done three projects in a niche will describe itself as a specialist practice to command premium rates. Risk: overpromising on complexity or scale.

**For Metier specifically:** The relevant risk is that deep capability in structured project delivery — schedule governance, risk management, owner's engineer functions — is communicated in language generic enough to be dismissed by a DC operator screening for sector-specific experience. The capability may be entirely transferable; the website may give no signal that the firm understands DC-specific challenges: N+1 redundancy commissioning, critical path compression under hyperscaler procurement cycles, or power infrastructure sequencing.
