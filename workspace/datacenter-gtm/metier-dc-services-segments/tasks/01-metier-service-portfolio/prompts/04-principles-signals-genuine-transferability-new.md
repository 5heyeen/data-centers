# Prompt 04: principles-signals-genuine-transferability-new

> **Prompt:** When assessing a consultancy's portfolio for a new vertical (like data centers), what signals indicate genuine transferability of services vs. superficial relabelling?

---

## Signals of Genuine Service Transferability vs. Superficial Relabelling

### 1. Sector-Specific Technical Fluency, Not Just Vocabulary
Genuine transferability shows up in technical depth. A consultant who actually understands data centers will speak naturally about Tier classification trade-offs, PUE targets, N+1 vs. 2N redundancy decisions, critical path risks around long-lead equipment (transformers, UPS systems, generators, chillers), and the interdependency between civil, MEP, and IT fit-out workstreams.

**Procurement signal:** Ask the consultant to walk through a risk register for a hyperscale build. If they produce a generic construction risk framework with "data center" substituted in, relabelling is apparent immediately.

### 2. Reference Projects With Verifiable Operational Outcomes
Genuine experience produces references — named clients, named projects, measurable outcomes (delivered on schedule, cost variance, uptime achieved at commissioning). Superficial repositioning produces anonymised case studies with vague descriptions.

**Procurement signal:** Request client references specifically from the DC vertical. Ask what phase the consultancy owned vs. advised on.

### 3. Talent That Has Crossed the Sector Boundary
If the partner pitching and the delivery team have actual data center programme delivery experience on their CVs, that is meaningful. The same applies to the delivery team, not just the pitch team.

**Procurement signal:** Request CVs for the proposed delivery team before contract signature.

### 4. Methodology Adapted to Sector Constraints
Data center projects have specific constraints: power procurement timelines, cooling technology lock-in decisions, concurrent maintainability requirements, commissioning sequencing with live infrastructure, ESG/PUE reporting tied to financing covenants. A genuinely capable consultancy has a methodology reflecting these.

**Procurement signal:** Ask how their methodology handles concurrent phased delivery where later phases must be built around live infrastructure.

### 5. Supply Chain and Ecosystem Relationships
Genuine sector experience produces working relationships with relevant specialist subcontractors, equipment vendors, commissioning agents, and independent engineers.

### Summary Heuristic
Ask the consultancy to identify the three highest-probability failure modes on a data center project of the type you are planning, and explain what their methodology does to mitigate each. Genuine capability produces specific, technically grounded answers.
