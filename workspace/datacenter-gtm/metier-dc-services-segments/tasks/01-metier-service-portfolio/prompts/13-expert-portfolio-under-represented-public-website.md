# Prompt 13: expert-portfolio-under-represented-public-website

> **Prompt:** What parts of a consultancy's portfolio are typically under-represented on their public website? Why might strong capabilities be invisible or poorly communicated externally?

---

## Under-Represented Portfolio Capabilities

**1. Confidential sector depth.** When a firm has done significant work for clients who cannot be named publicly — government, defense, PE portfolio companies under NDA — that experience simply does not appear on the website.

**2. Operational/delivery capabilities vs. advisory framing.** Firms lead with strategy and advisory language because it positions them upstream and commands higher rates. But actual revenue and headcount may be concentrated in programme management, PMO setup, and construction oversight — hard, execution-heavy work that sounds unglamorous in marketing copy.

**3. Sub-service specialisations.** A website may list "project management" as a single service line, hiding genuine depth in schedule risk analysis, earned value management, contract governance, or commissioning management — distinctions that matter enormously to sophisticated buyers.

**4. Individual partner expertise.** Partners often carry capabilities the firm has never codified as a service line. A partner who spent a decade in energy infrastructure brings knowledge marketed generically as "capital projects."

**5. Volume and scale of delivery.** Websites rarely communicate how many projects a firm has run concurrently, total CAPEX supervised, or team size deployed — the indicators an operator uses to assess whether the firm can handle a large programme.

**6. Niche regulatory competence.** Firms working in highly regulated sectors (nuclear, telecom, defense-adjacent infrastructure) often use deliberately generic language to maintain positioning flexibility.

**Core reason:** Websites are written for business development, not capability disclosure. They are optimised for initial contact and brand positioning, not for the due diligence a sophisticated buyer actually performs.
