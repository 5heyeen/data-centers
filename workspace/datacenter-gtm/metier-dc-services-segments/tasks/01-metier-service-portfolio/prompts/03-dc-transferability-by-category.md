# Prompt 03: dc-transferability-by-category

> **Prompt:** Which service categories have highest DC transferability?

---

## Service Category Transferability Ratings

**1. Owner's Engineer / PMC — HIGH**
Data center projects are routinely executed by financial investors, real estate developers, and energy companies entering the sector for the first time; they structurally lack in-house technical representation, making owner's engineer the single most universally demanded advisory role across all operator archetypes.

**2. Project Execution & Project Management — HIGH**
DC construction is a capital-intensive, schedule-driven discipline with parallel workstreams (civil, power, cooling, IT fit-out) and tight commissioning windows; professional PM is required regardless of operator background, and the competency gap is acute because DC operators rarely carry large internal project delivery teams.

**3. Procurement & Contract Advisory — HIGH**
Long-lead equipment (generators, transformers, UPS, cooling systems), complex scope-split decisions (EPC vs. EPCM vs. CM), and security-clearance constraints on contractor pools make procurement structurally harder in data centers than in most sectors Metier already serves, and operators almost universally lack internal procurement expertise.

**4. Portfolio & Project Owner Governance — HIGH**
Institutional and PE investors require milestone-gated reporting, stage-gate processes, and portfolio-level dashboards as a condition of continued capital deployment; this demand is investor-driven rather than operator-driven, making it non-discretionary and highly repeatable across funded operators.

**5. Strategy Advisory — MEDIUM**
Strategy engagements are high-value but episodic and situational — most useful for new market entrants defining positioning or established operators pivoting to AI/HPC — and DC-specific strategy requires sector knowledge Metier is still building, meaning the service transfers well in form but requires credentialing investment to win mandates.

**6. Early-Phase Advisory (Concept Selection & Optimization) — MEDIUM**
Front-end advisory on site selection, power planning, and phasing decisions maps naturally onto Metier's existing VPU/PDRI frameworks, but DC technology choices (liquid cooling, tier architecture, redundancy design) carry enough domain-specific content that Metier must supplement its process toolkit with DC engineering expertise to deliver credibly.

**7. Competence Development & Training — MEDIUM**
Rapidly scaling DC operators face real workforce capability gaps and Metier Academy has the delivery infrastructure, but training is typically a follow-on relationship built after a delivery engagement and is rarely the initial entry point into a new sector account.

**8. Digital Advisory — LOW**
Digital transformation services are applicable only in narrow DC contexts (DCIM platforms, BIM for construction, customer portal development) and face stiff competition from IT consultancies with deeper domain credibility; the overlap between Metier's digital capability and DC operator priorities is narrow and situational.
