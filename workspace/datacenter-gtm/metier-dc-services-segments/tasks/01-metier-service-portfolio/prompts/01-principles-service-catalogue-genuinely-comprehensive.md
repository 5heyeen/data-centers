# Prompt 01: principles-service-catalogue-genuinely-comprehensive

> **Prompt:** What makes a professional services firm's service catalogue genuinely comprehensive vs. superficially broad? What distinguishes firms that can truly execute across many service lines from those that list services they can't deliver?

---

## What Makes a Professional Services Firm's Service Catalogue Genuinely Comprehensive vs. Superficially Broad

### The Core Distinction: Depth of Delivery Capability

A superficially broad catalogue is a marketing artifact. A genuinely comprehensive one is an operational reality. The distinction lies not in what a firm lists, but in whether it can staff, govern, and deliver each service line under real client pressure.

### Diagnostic Criteria for Genuine Comprehensiveness

**1. Dedicated Practice Leadership per Service Line**
Firms that truly execute across many services have named, experienced leaders for each practice — people with track records in that specific domain, not generalists wearing multiple hats. A red flag is when the same three partners appear as the lead contact across six "different" service lines.

**2. Proprietary Methodology and Tools**
Real capability leaves artifacts: frameworks, templates, playbooks, and proprietary assessments built through repeated delivery. A firm that has genuinely done cost estimation 40 times has developed a cost model. When evaluating a catalogue, the question is: can they show me the methodology, not just describe it?

**3. Referenceable Client History per Service**
The most reliable test is whether a firm can produce references — real clients, real engagements — for each service line independently.

**4. Staffing Depth, Not Just Headcount**
A firm of 200 people claiming 12 service lines may have only 2-3 people who can actually deliver in several of those lines. Genuine comprehensiveness requires that each service line can survive the simultaneous absence of one or two key people.

**5. Integration Without Conflict**
Truly comprehensive firms have figured out how their services connect. Superficially broad firms often create internal competition between service lines or deliver each in a silo.

### Common Patterns of Superficial Breadth

- **Acqui-hire bloat:** Adding a boutique's services to the catalogue before integration is complete
- **Subcontractor laundering:** Listing services routinely subcontracted without disclosure
- **Horizon expansion without depth:** Adding "implementation support" to follow a trend without the delivery infrastructure
- **Vanity capability statements:** Services described at such high abstraction they could apply to anything

### The Actionable Standard

A genuinely comprehensive service catalogue passes the **"deploy test"**: for any listed service, could the firm deploy a capable, dedicated team within 30 days to a client that selected them solely for that service? If not, the catalogue is aspirational, not operational.
