# Prompt 03: principles-service-line-marketed-vs-delivered

> **Prompt:** What is the difference between a service line a firm markets vs. a capability it actually delivers at scale? How can you tell from public information whether a listed service is core or peripheral?

---

## Core vs. Peripheral Services: How to Distinguish What a Firm Actually Delivers at Scale

### The Fundamental Distinction

A **marketed service line** is what appears on the website and in proposals. A **delivered-at-scale capability** is what the firm repeatedly executes with dedicated staff, repeatable methodology, and a client track record. Most professional services firms market broadly to avoid excluding opportunities, while delivering deeply in a narrower band.

### Signals That a Service Is Core (Delivered at Scale)

1. **Headcount and role specificity** — Job postings are highly reliable. If a firm consistently advertises for specialists (not generalists) in a domain, that domain is core.
2. **Named methodology or toolset** — Core capabilities have proprietary frameworks, named models, or branded tools. Peripheral services are described generically ("we support clients with...").
3. **Case study depth and specificity** — Core services generate case studies with measurable outcomes. Peripheral services generate vague references.
4. **Dedicated practice leadership** — Core services have a named partner/lead with a public profile (LinkedIn, conference appearances, published articles).
5. **Volume and repeatability** — A capability delivered at scale implies repeated engagements across multiple clients and project types.

### Signals That a Service Is Peripheral

1. **Listed last or under "also"** — Services at the end of a capabilities list or under catch-all headings are often aspirational.
2. **Higher level of abstraction** — "Strategic advisory" described alongside "milestone planning and earned value management" is likely sold relationship-by-relationship.
3. **No dedicated hiring** — Absence of job postings in a domain over 12–24 months signals no investment in that capability.
4. **Subcontractor or partner language** — "In partnership with," "drawing on our network," or "together with specialist advisors" signal the firm is a broker, not an operator, in that domain.

### Application to Metier

Metier's verifiable core — based on headcount, methodology (OEC), case study depth, and hiring patterns — is **project governance, cost control, and owner-side project management** on large capital projects. Services like ESG reporting, digital transformation advisory, or organizational change are more likely marketed than delivered with the same depth. In DC pursuits, Metier can credibly own the owner's engineer and PMO role, but should be careful not to over-promise on domains where it would need to subcontract.
