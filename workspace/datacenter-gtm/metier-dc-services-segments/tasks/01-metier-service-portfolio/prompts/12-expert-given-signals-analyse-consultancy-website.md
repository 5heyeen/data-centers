## Methodology for Analysing a Consultancy Website to Produce a Reliable Service Catalogue

### Step-by-Step Approach

**1. Start with the navigation structure, not the content**
The menu hierarchy reveals how the firm thinks about its portfolio. Services listed at the top level are what the firm believes drives revenue and recognition. Services buried under sub-menus or "also" sections are likely peripheral. Note what is absent as much as what is present.

**2. Extract all explicitly named service lines**
List every service name and sub-service that appears anywhere on the site — service pages, about pages, case study tags, team bio descriptions. Create a master list with the exact language used. Marketing language choices (e.g., "prosjekteierstyring" vs. "PMO") reveal positioning intent.

**3. Score each service against the reliability hierarchy (from prompt 11)**
For each listed service, ask:
- Is there a substantive case study? (Named client, measurable outcome)
- Are there team bios with specific experience in this domain?
- Is there published methodology or intellectual property?
- Are there relevant certifications?
- Or is it only a logo and a paragraph of description?

Services with only a paragraph of description and a client logo are likely aspirational. Services backed by case studies, named practitioners, and methodology documentation are likely operational.

**4. Cross-reference with hiring signals**
Current and recent job postings are one of the most reliable external validators. If the firm is hiring "senior project controls specialist" but not listing project controls as a named service — or vice versa — there is a gap between operational reality and marketing. Job ads also reveal which service lines are growing (multiple open roles) vs. static.

**5. Check the team page for practice area clustering**
If team members are tagged by service or sector, the clustering reveals where real depth sits. A firm with 15 project managers tagged "infrastructure & energy" and 2 tagged "digital transformation" has an obvious capability asymmetry regardless of how the service pages are written.

**6. Flag for supplementary research**
After the website analysis, identify which services require external validation (job boards, LinkedIn, news) before inclusion in the confirmed catalogue vs. the aspirational catalogue.

### Output Structure
Produce two lists: **Confirmed Services** (backed by multiple reliability signals) and **Marketed Services** (described on website but lacking substantive evidence of depth). This is more honest and actionable than a single flat catalogue.
