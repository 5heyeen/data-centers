## Mapping Service Categories to the Capital Project Lifecycle

The standard capital project lifecycle: **Concept/Feasibility → Pre-FEED/FEED → Detailed Design → Procurement → Construction/Execution → Commissioning → Operations**

### Early-Phase (Concept → FEED)
- **Strategy & Advisory** — market entry analysis, feasibility assessment, investment framing
- **Early-Phase Advisory / VPU** — concept selection, site evaluation, phasing strategy, PDRI assessments
- **Procurement & Contract Management** — contract strategy selection (EPC vs EPCM vs CM), long-lead equipment planning
- **Owner's Engineer** — independent cost estimation, FEED design review, technical due diligence
- **Risk Management** — qualitative risk workshops, quantitative schedule/cost risk analysis to inform contingency

### Execution-Phase (Detailed Design → Construction)
- **PMO / Programme Management** — dominant phase; schedule control, cost control, progress reporting, interface management, change management
- **Owner's Engineer** — contractor oversight, design verification, constructability reviews, RFI/variation management, quality assurance
- **Procurement & Contract Management** — tender packages, supplier qualification, bid evaluation, contract administration
- **Digital & Technology** — BIM coordination, PMIS deployment, document control
- **Risk Management** — risk register maintenance, contingency drawdown governance

### Close-Out & Commissioning
- **Owner's Engineer** — witnessing commissioning tests, punch list management, handover documentation, O&M manual review
- **PMO / Programme Management** — close-out reporting, lessons learned, post-project review

### Operations & Beyond
- **Training & Competence Development** — operator training, PM upskilling for ongoing capital works
- **Digital & Technology** — DCIM/PMIS transition to operational teams, reporting automation
- **Portfolio Governance** — ongoing multi-site oversight, standardised reporting for institutional investors

### Key Insight for DC Context
Data center projects compress this lifecycle significantly (12–36 months from FID to commissioning) and often run phases concurrently. This puts extreme pressure on the early handoffs — decisions made poorly in FEED propagate quickly to construction. Owner's Engineer and PMO services that can span the full lifecycle without restart are consequently much more valuable than phase-specific specialists.
