# Prompt 02: principles-consultancies-organise-service-lines

> **Prompt:** How do management/project consultancies typically organise their service lines — by industry, capability, engagement type, or client need? What are the trade-offs of each approach?

---

## How Management/Project Consultancies Organise Their Service Lines

Consultancies typically organise around one of four primary dimensions, and most mature firms blend two or more.

### 1. By Industry (Vertical/Sector Alignment)

Firms like McKinsey, Roland Berger, and Turner & Townsend maintain dedicated industry practices. Turner & Townsend, notably, organises heavily by sector (data centres, infrastructure, real estate).

**Advantages:** Deep domain credibility; proprietary sector benchmarks; targeted BD; pricing power.
**Trade-offs:** Siloing of capability expertise; revenue volatility if a sector softens; limited cross-sector flexibility.

### 2. By Capability (Horizontal/Functional Organisation)

Firms like BCG and many pure PM firms organise around what they do: strategy, programme management, procurement, change management, risk advisory.

**Advantages:** Centres of excellence; faster skill deepening; staff deployable across sectors; easier methodology management.
**Trade-offs:** Generalist perception from clients; high internal coordination costs; unclear BD ownership.

### 3. By Engagement Type (Offering/Product Line)

Some consultancies separate advisory/strategy, implementation/delivery, managed services, and training. Accenture and PwC use this logic at the macro level.

**Advantages:** Clean economics per engagement type; clear client expectations; easier to productise and scale.
**Trade-offs:** Fragments client relationship across teams; staff identity tied to type rather than domain knowledge.

### 4. By Client Need / Pain Point

Boutiques or thought-leadership firms organise around named problems: "capital project delivery," "cost reduction," "ESG compliance."

**Advantages:** Client-centric framing; maps directly to procurement decisions; naturally cross-functional.
**Trade-offs:** Pain points shift over time; overlap between teams; difficult to build stable staffing.

### The Dominant Hybrid in Mid-Size Firms

Most mid-size consultancies (where Metier sits) use a **capability-led structure with sector overlays**: core service lines (programme management, PMO, procurement, risk) are the stable backbone, while sector-specific positioning (energy, real estate, data centres) is applied as a go-to-market layer — in proposals, marketing, and BD — without reorganising the firm around it. This preserves utilisation flexibility while enabling credible sector narratives.
