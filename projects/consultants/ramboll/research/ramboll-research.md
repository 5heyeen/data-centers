## 1. The Big Picture — What Ramboll Is in Data Centers

Ramboll is a Danish-headquartered global engineering and consulting firm (~16,000 employees, 35 countries). Its data center practice is built on three pillars:

1. **Ramboll base** — Sustainability, environmental, building services, and energy engineering applied to DC projects
2. **EYP Mission Critical Facilities (EYP MCF)** — Acquired January 31, 2022. US-headquartered mission-critical design specialist. ~50 professionals at acquisition; now operates as "EYP Mission Critical Facilities, Part of Ramboll" with separate website (eypmcfinc.com). Claims 70M+ sq ft of critically powered space designed over 25+ years.
3. **i3 Solutions Group** — Acquired September 2024. UK/Singapore sustainability and design consultancy founded by Ed Ansett. Adds European and Asian market depth plus net-zero DC expertise.

Combined claim: **"Hundreds of dedicated data centre design experts globally."**

## 2. The "EYP Playbook"

### Important clarification
"EYP Architecture+Engineering" and "EYP Mission Critical Facilities" are related but different entities. The firm Ramboll acquired is **EYP MCF**, not the broader EYP A+E. EYP MCF was founded 1996 within Einhorn Yaffee Prescott; acquired by HP 2007; spun back out via MBO 2018; backed by TA Associates PE May 2021; acquired by Ramboll January 2022.

### There is no single "playbook" document
EYP MCF does NOT publish a document titled "playbook." The term refers to their integrated delivery methodology, which consists of:

| Named Offering | What It Is |
|----------------|------------|
| **Hybrid IT and Adaptive Stack Strategy** | Strategic IT advisory framework — build/lease/colo/cloud analysis for enterprise clients |
| **Sourcing and Demand Analysis** | Multistep methodology for multiyear DC sourcing solutions |
| **Operational and Reliability Risk Assessment** | Named service line covering reliability, ops risk, capacity, infrastructure condition |
| **Probability Risk Analysis (PRA)** | Named methodology for site auditing existing infrastructure |
| **IUE (Infrastructure Usage Effectiveness)** | Industry metric co-developed by EYP MCF's Yigit Bulut with The Green Grid/TGGC; extends PUE |
| **Net Zero Data Center Roadmap** | Strategic sustainability framework published September 2025 (Ramboll, led by Ed Ansett) |

### What the playbook covers (technical scope)
- Tier III and Tier IV design (Uptime Institute) — claims first Tier IV facilities in US, Saudi Arabia, China
- Cooling architectures: adiabatic, chilled water, evaporative, liquid immersion, rear-door heat exchangers
- Modular/containerized delivery — documented at scale (700K sq ft Dallas colo; 45 EcoPODs)
- CFD thermal modeling as standard practice
- HPC/supercomputing design heritage (NCAR-Wyoming, NCSA Illinois)

### Key publications
- 2021: "Infrastructure Sustainability Options and Revenue Opportunities for Data Centres" (EYP MCF + i3)
- 2025: "Developing Sustainable Data Centres: A Strategic Roadmap to Achieve Net Zero Carbon" (Ramboll, Climate Week NYC)
- IUE white paper (The Green Grid/TGGC)

## 6. Competitive Positioning

### Ramboll's core competitive claim
**"International premier league of data center consultancies"** — positioning against Arup, WSP, Arcadis with differentiation on:
1. Mission-critical depth (via EYP MCF)
2. Sustainability/net-zero leadership (via i3 and Ramboll environmental practice)
3. Integrated strategy-to-commissioning scope under one brand
4. Nordic waste heat + district heating integration experience

### Positioning relative to competitors
| Firm | Ramboll's Positioning vs. Them |
|------|--------------------------------|
| **Arup** | Similar engineering depth; Ramboll differentiates on sustainability and the EYP MCF strategic advisory framework |
| **WSP** | Most direct global competitor; Ramboll smaller but more specialist-weighted |
| **Arcadis** | Different positioning — Arcadis owns the PM/cost/program management space; Ramboll owns engineering |
| **Aurecon** | Geographic differentiation — Aurecon strong in APAC; Ramboll stronger in US + Nordics |
| **Sweco** | Direct Nordic competitor — Sweco likely has stronger named Norwegian DC references |
| **Norconsult, Cowi** | Direct Nordic competitors |

### Key weaknesses
1. **Limited European/Norwegian named project references** (combined practice is new as of i3 acquisition in Sept 2024)
2. **Hyperscaler client relationships opaque** — may exist but not publicly visible
3. **AI/liquid cooling technical currency not demonstrated in published frameworks**
4. **No owner's representative or PM service line** — forces clients to split services between Ramboll and a PM firm
5. **Integration recency of i3** — European practice less than 2 years old

## 7. Strategic Implications for Metier

### 7.1 Metier's competitive posture vs. Ramboll

**Metier and Ramboll do not directly compete.** They occupy complementary — not overlapping — roles:
- **Ramboll/EYP:** Engineering design and strategic IT advisory
- **Metier:** Owner-side project management advisory

The most productive framing: **Metier may recommend Ramboll (or a competitor) as the engineering sub-consultant on projects where Metier holds the PM mandate.**

### 7.2 Where Metier has a durable competitive advantage

1. **Norwegian PM methodology and project culture fluency**
2. **Owner-side role independence** (no conflict of interest between design and PM)
3. **Norwegian local operator relationships** (if established)
4. **Norwegian language and regulatory fluency at working level**
5. **Stakeholder and permitting expertise** for Norwegian public-sector interfaces

### 7.3 Where Metier is genuinely at risk from Ramboll

1. **Strategic DC advisory mandates** where a client wants a single firm for strategy → design → commissioning (EYP MCF's Hybrid IT framework)
2. **Sustainability-led advisory** where a client wants combined sustainability framework + engineering scope (Ramboll net-zero roadmap + i3/EYP engineering)

### 7.4 Partnership opportunities

| Scenario | Metier Role | Ramboll Role |
|----------|-------------|-------------|
| Hyperscaler Norwegian campus | Owner's PM advisor, local PM lead | Engineering design sub-consultant |
| Sustainability/waste heat DC project | Owner's PM + Norwegian regulatory lead | Engineering design + district heating integration |
| Colo operator Norwegian expansion | Owner's PM + stakeholder management | Engineering design + modular delivery |
| AI campus development in Norway | Owner's PM + local operator relationships | AI/high-density engineering design |

### 7.5 Watch list — signals of Ramboll's Norway market entry
- Ramboll hires for DC-specific roles in Norwegian offices (finn.no, LinkedIn)
- First named Norwegian DC project for Ramboll in public press
- Ramboll participation in Norwegian Data Centre Industry association
- Ed Ansett / i3 thought leadership at Nordic DC events
- Extension of EYP MCF leadership travel to Norway
