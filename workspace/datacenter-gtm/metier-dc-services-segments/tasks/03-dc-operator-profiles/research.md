# Research: DC Operator Type Profiles

**Executed:** 2026-04-18
**Chunks:** 3 parallel operator profile chunks

---

## Operator Type 1: Hyperscalers

**Who:** Google, Microsoft, AWS, Meta — building proprietary DCs at scale.

**Metier opportunity: NOT A TARGET.**
Hyperscalers build internal owner's engineer and PMC capability at scale. They run multi-billion-euro programs with proprietary design standards, in-house procurement, and global PM teams. External advisors fill narrow specialist gaps (local permitting, regulatory navigation) but never lead strategically. Vendor qualification alone takes multi-year investment. Realistic only if Metier partners with a firm already on a hyperscaler framework.

**What they need from external advisors:** Norwegian regulatory navigation (konsesjon, Statnett queue, local planning), local community engagement — not PM or OE services.

---

## Operator Type 2: Colocation Operators

**Who:** Green Mountain, Stack/DigiPlex, Lefdal Mine, Bulk Infrastructure, Colobridge — selling power/space/cooling to enterprise customers.

**Metier opportunity: PRIMARY TARGET.**
Commercially-led organisations building complex bespoke facilities with lean internal teams. The structural mismatch between aggressive MW expansion ambitions and execution capacity is exactly where Metier fits.

**Profile:**
- Small leadership teams (CEO + VP Construction + CFO); PE or infrastructure fund backing common
- Multi-site pipelines with portfolio governance needs
- Procurement-by-relationship; strong Norwegian market reference transfers across the peer group
- ESG positioning is core to their commercial proposition (sustainability, renewable energy, waste heat)

**Priority services:** Owner's Engineer, Contract & Procurement, Project Execution & PM, Portfolio Governance

**Entry point:** Contract packaging advisory before EPC tender; OE role from EPC contract award

**Norwegian examples:** Green Mountain (largest Norwegian colo, Rennesøy + Enebakk), Stack/DigiPlex (Ulven, multiple sites), Lefdal Mine (underground DC in Fjord Norway), Bulk (Ballangen, 1.5 GW ambition)

---

## Operator Type 3: PE-Backed DC Developers (Finance-First)

**Who:** Finance/consulting-background founders with PE capital building greenfield DCs. Norwegian examples: Fossefall (500 MW target, SPV-per-site structure), Kitebrook (130 MW pipeline, ~1–4 staff), Bifrost Edge.

**Metier opportunity: HIGHEST PRIORITY TARGET.**
Classic Pattern A: extreme ambition-to-staff ratio, zero PM/procurement function, investors imposing governance requirements the operator cannot yet meet.

**Profile:**
- Finance-first leadership (founders from PE, consulting, real estate — not construction)
- Aggressive MW targets announced publicly
- SPV-per-site capital structure → each project needs its own governance and reporting
- Milestone-gated capital: PE investors deploy tranches tied to FID, groundbreak, commissioning milestones
- Zero internal project management or procurement capability

**Trigger events for buying:** FID imminence; investor due diligence process; EPC tender launch; PE capital round closed

**Investor-imposed requirements that create demand:** Quarterly milestone reporting, board CAPEX approval thresholds, ESG mandates (EU Taxonomy), independent technical validation for drawdown requests

**Priority services:** Owner's Engineer, Contract & Procurement, Project Execution & PM, Uncertainty Analysis (UA), Portfolio Governance

**Entry point:** Pre-tender contract packaging advisory; OE at EPC contract award; UA at FID

---

## Operator Type 4: High-Security Operators

**Who:** DC operators targeting government, defense, NATO clients. Norwegian examples: Skygard (Hafslund/Telenor/HitecVision JV, NOK 2.4B OSL1), Lefdal Mine (sovereign cloud positioning).

**Metier opportunity: HIGH — but with hard constraints.**

**Profile:**
- Strategy built around sovereignty, security, sustainability ("3 S's" — Skygard)
- Sikkerhetsklarering requirement filters contractor pool severely — cleared contractors are scarce, suppressing competitive tension and raising unit prices
- Contract packages must separate cleared and non-cleared scopes from outset — otherwise entire project is security-constrained
- Construction timeline driven partly by clearance processing times (weeks to 6+ months per level)
- State-adjacent ownership common (Hafslund, Telenor) — governance requirements are institutional

**Metier's hard limit:** Metier cannot hold clearances or access classified areas unless individual staff hold personal clearances. Metier designs the system around security constraints but does not operate inside them.

**Strategic option:** Building a cadre of cleared PMs (Metier staff with personal sikkerhetsklarering) would be a genuine market differentiator for Pattern B — worth evaluating as a medium-term capability investment.

**Priority services:** Contract & Procurement (cleared contractor pool management), Project Execution & PM, Owner's Engineer, Portfolio Governance

**Entry point:** Contract strategy for cleared-vs-non-cleared scope split before tender launch

## Operator Type 5: Utility/Energy Company Spin-offs

**Who:** Energy companies leveraging power assets, land, and grid connections to enter DCs. Norwegian examples: Eidsiva Lighthavn (Gjøvik + Rudshøgda), Eviny (BKK spin-off), Statkraft Ventures.

**Metier opportunity: MEDIUM.**

**Profile:**
- Structural advantage: grid, power, land, sovereignty narrative, renewable energy credentials
- Execution gap: lack DC-specific PM methodology, long-lead equipment procurement expertise, MEP package management
- Decision cycles are slow — utility governance is layered; investment decisions go through multiple board levels
- Often partner with commercial operators rather than build independently (Eviny model)

**Priority services:** Early-Phase Advisory (converting power/land assets into viable DC concept), Strategy Advisory, Owner's Engineer / PMC once build approved

**Entry point:** Pre-FID concept selection and strategy — natural Metier entry given VPU methodology

**Realistic assessment:** Strong conceptual fit but long sales cycles and conservative governance structures. Best targeted opportunistically through energy sector relationships Metier already has, not as a proactive first-priority segment.

---

## Operator Type 6: Enterprise DC Operators

**Who:** Large Norwegian corporates managing their own DC infrastructure.

**Metier opportunity: LOW — pursue opportunistically.**
Enterprise-owned DCs are a declining volume segment (trend is toward cloud/colo migration). When projects occur, they are IT-department-led, cost-center-driven, often subject to formal procurement rules. Slow and hard to win cold.

**Exception:** High-security public sector (defense, intelligence, police) does build owned DCs but has its own procurement constraints (identical to Pattern B). If Metier can access this through existing public sector relationships, it's worth pursuing.

| Segment | Opportunity | Priority | Entry Approach |
|---------|------------|----------|----------------|
| PE-Backed DC Developers | HIGHEST | 1st | Direct outreach; PE sponsor channel; pre-tender window |
| Colocation Operators | HIGH | 2nd | Norwegian market references; relationship-driven |
| High-Security Operators | HIGH (constrained) | 3rd | Contract strategy for cleared-scope split |
| Utility/Energy Spin-offs | MEDIUM | 4th | Existing energy sector relationships; pre-FID |
| Enterprise DC | LOW | 5th | Opportunistic via existing relationships only |
| Hyperscalers | NONE | Not targeted | Partner-only; not a standalone target |
