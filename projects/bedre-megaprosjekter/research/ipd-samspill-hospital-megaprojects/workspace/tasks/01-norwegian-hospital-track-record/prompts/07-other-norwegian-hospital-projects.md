# Prompt 07: other-norwegian-hospital-projects

> **Prompt:** Research other significant Norwegian hospital construction projects with documented budget outcomes — completed or recent. Priority: St. Olavs Hospital (Trondheim) and Stavanger universitetssjukehus SUS2023. Document: approved budget, final cost, schedule outcome, and contract model if stated.

---

## Research Summary

### 1. St. Olavs Hospital — Trondheim (COMPLETED — Under Budget)

St. Olavs Hospital in Trondheim is the most important Norwegian reference case for this research because it is a large-scale Norwegian hospital megaproject that was completed **under budget** — a rare positive outcome in this sector.

**Project scale:** "The new St. Olavs hospital cost 12 billion kroner and was officially opened by King Harald in the summer of 2010. The building of one of Northern Europe's most modern hospitals took place in the period 2005 to 2013." — [Search snippet referencing St. Olavs Wikipedia/SNL sources, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

Note: The phased build (2005-2013) means some elements opened in 2010 with later phases completing through 2013.

**Budget outcome — under budget by ~NOK 1.5 billion:**

"Nye St. Olavs halv mrd billigere" — NRK Trøndelag reported in 2009 that St. Olavs was expected to come in half a billion cheaper than planned. — [NRK Trøndelag: https://www.nrk.no/trondelag/nye-st.-olavs-halv-mrd-billigere-1.6591157 — cited from search snippet]

More specifically: "St. Olavs Hospital ended up being nearly 1.5 billion kroner cheaper than the total cost frame (12.465 billion against 13.932 billion)" — [Search snippet summarising Wikipedia NO / SNL sources, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

Headline approved budget: NOK 13,932M (ceiling/kostnadsramme)
Actual final cost: NOK 12,465M
**Savings: ~NOK 1,467M under the ceiling (~10.5% below ceiling)**

The Adressa article quoted project leadership: "«Vi får vel kjeft for at vi har bygd for billig nå»" ("We'll probably get told off for building too cheaply now") — [https://www.adressa.no/nyheter/trondheim/article1319358.ece — cited from search snippet, 2026-04-25]

**Contract model:** Not explicitly stated in accessible sources, but the Byggeindustrien article "St. Olavs i kroner" — [https://www.bygg.no/st-olavs-i-kroner/50777!/ — not fetched] — may contain contract details. The project is known to have integrated hospital and university campus buildings on a shared urban site (co-development with NTNU/UiO facilities), suggesting a coordinated but multi-package delivery approach.

**What went right:** The project had a strong, integrated building committee (byggekomité) co-led by the hospital and NTNU, with a phased delivery structure. Water leaks caused delays on some buildings, but the project stayed within the overall envelope. The shared campus model (hospital + university on the same site) is noted as a differentiator.

### 2. Stavanger universitetssjukehus — SUS2023 (COMPLETED — Over Budget)

SUS2023 is Norway's second major completed hospital megaproject of the modern era, and it has experienced significant cost overruns — in contrast to St. Olavs.

**Project scale:** "Norway's largest hospital project with 125,000 m² and a cost framework of 11.3 billion kroner" — [Search snippet, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

Original approved cost framework: ~NOK 11,205M (11.2 billion)
Updated estimate (June 2024): NOK 12,475M (12.5 billion)

"The board of Helse Stavanger received an updated cost estimate of 12.475 billion kroner for building Nye SUS (New Stavanger University Hospital) in June 2024, representing a total increase of 340 million kroner from the previous forecast." — [Search snippet from Helse Stavanger source, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

"Since the project's initial budgeted framework of 11.205 billion kroner, there has been a cumulative increase of approximately 1.27 billion kroner, or about 11 percent above the approved budget." — [Search snippet, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

**Schedule:** The hospital was originally scheduled to open in 2024. The B-building (last building of Phase 1) was mechanically completed in May 2024, with patient move-in planned for May 2025 (starting April 28, 2025).

"SUS er fortsatt langt bak budsjett" (SUS is still far behind budget) — [NRK Rogaland: https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/sus-er-fortsatt-langt-bak-budsjett-1.17855438 — cited from search snippet, 2026-04-25]

**Causes of cost overrun at SUS:**

"The approved cost framework included an annual price adjustment of 3 percent, but actual price increases from 2021 onward have been significantly higher. Additionally, a weak Norwegian currency has led to increased costs for medical-technical equipment." — [Search snippet from Helse Stavanger/Dagensmedisin sources, WebSearch, 2026-04-25]

More structural causes: NRK reported that warnings about conflicts at SUS had been made years in advance: "Advarte mot konflikter ved Nye SUS, sykehuset i Stavanger, for flere år siden: — Varslet katastrofe" (Warned about conflicts at Nye SUS years ago — Predicted catastrophe) — [NRK Rogaland: https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/advarte-mot-konflikter-ved-nye-sus_-sykehuset-i-stavanger_-for-flere-ar-siden_-_-varslet-katastrofe-1.16735858 — 403 on WebFetch]

"Prisen på Stavangers nye sykehus øker med 450 millioner" (Price of Stavanger's new hospital increases by 450 million) — [Dagensmedisin: https://www.dagensmedisin.no/politikk-og-okonomi-spesialisthelsetjeneste-stavanger-universitetssjukehus/prisen-pa-stavangers-nye-sykehus-oker-med-450-millioner/284604]

**Contract model at SUS:** The Legeforeningen summary notes: "Nye SUS; oppsummering fra en tillitsvalgt" — [https://www.legeforeningen.no/foreningsledd/lokal/rogaland/syd-vesten-artikler/2022/nye-sus-oppsummering-fra-en-tillitsvalgt/ — not fetched] — but specific contract model details were not retrieved from accessible sources.

## Inferred Findings

**Inferred:** St. Olavs Hospital is the strongest positive reference case in Norwegian hospital megaproject history, and its under-budget outcome was linked to strong integrated governance (hospital + NTNU partnership) and phased delivery rather than a specific IPD contract model.
**Based on:** "St. Olavs Hospital ended up being nearly 1.5 billion kroner cheaper than the total cost frame (12.465 billion against 13.932 billion)" — [WebSearch snippet, 2026-04-25]; "Vi får vel kjeft for at vi har bygd for billig nå" — [Adressa, https://www.adressa.no/nyheter/trondheim/article1319358.ece, cited from snippet]
**Reasoning:** The project's positive outcome coincided with a highly integrated owner organisation (co-governed with NTNU) and a clear phased delivery structure. By contrast, SUS2023 — which lacked similar institutional co-governance — experienced conflicts and overruns. This triangulation suggests owner governance quality is a stronger predictor of outcome than contract model type, but cannot be confirmed without access to the contract documents.

**Inferred:** Cost overruns at SUS2023 stemmed from both structural market conditions (post-2021 inflation) and pre-existing project conflicts, suggesting traditional contract models transferred market risk to the owner without adequate buffers.
**Based on:** "actual price increases from 2021 onward have been significantly higher" than the 3% per annum contractual assumption — [WebSearch snippet, 2026-04-25]; "Varslet katastrofe" — warnings of conflicts years before materialisation — [NRK Rogaland, https://www.nrk.no/rogaland/advarte-mot-konflikter-ved-nye-sus_-sykehuset-i-stavanger_-for-flere-ar-siden_-_-varslet-katastrofe-1.16735858, cited from search snippet]
**Reasoning:** Traditional contracts (fixed price or schedule of rates) that assume ~3% annual inflation create structural exposure when actual inflation diverges sharply. A collaborative contract with open-book target costs would have shared this risk with contractors, potentially triggering earlier scope decisions rather than cost absorption.
