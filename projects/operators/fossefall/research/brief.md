## 1. Company Snapshot

Fossefall AS is a Norwegian AI infrastructure startup founded in June 2025, targeting 500 MW of "AI factory" capacity across Norway and Sweden by 2030. The company was launched by a finance-first team — CEO Øyvind Laugen Vesterdal comes from Valinor, Glacier Link, and Norsk Solar — and has raised ~NOK 60M in seed capital from a syndicate of angels and family offices including tech entrepreneur Stian Rustad (24SevenOffice/Done.ai), Swedish billionaire Roger Samuelsson, Norwegian "wind power king" Lars Helge Helvig (Valinor), and US AI firm Seekr Technologies (AMD Ventures, 15.6% stake).

Fossefall has 6-7 employees and an 11+ site pipeline totaling 385+ MW. Their first site (Fyresdal, 5.5 MW acquired from Norsk Data AS) is targeting operations in 2026. Major projects in discussion include Harpefoss (~30 MW, ~NOK 3B) and Rollag (15 MW, >NOK 1B), both facing local community opposition. The company operates an SPV-per-site project finance model and plans to raise "several billion NOK" in institutional capital in 2026.

The core thesis is converting Norway's cheap renewable hydropower and cold climate into Europe's AI compute infrastructure, with data sovereignty as a key differentiator. Seekr Technologies is both anchor customer (36-month capacity reservation) and strategic investor, providing revenue visibility but also customer concentration risk.

**Why this matters now:** Fossefall is approaching the critical transition from deal-making to execution. Financial close on first major projects is targeted for 2026, but the company has almost no project delivery capability. The gap between ambition (500 MW, 11 sites) and resources (6-7 people, ~NOK 60M) is the defining risk — and the defining opportunity for Metier.

## 3. Funding & Investor Context

### Capital Structure
- **Total funding:** ~NOK 60M seed capital
- **Key investors:** Seekr Technologies (15.6%), Stian Rustad, Ravenger/Roger Samuelsson, Valinor/Lars Helge Helvig, Done.ai (2.4%)
- **Fund type & implications:** All angel/family office/strategic — no PE or institutional investors yet. Patient capital but lightweight governance. The planned multi-billion raise will bring institutional investors with stricter requirements.

### Investor-Driven Constraints
- **Timeline pressure:** Seekr's 36-month capacity reservation = operational AI capacity needed by ~end 2026. This is the hardest near-term deadline.
- **Governance overhead:** Currently light. Will increase dramatically with institutional capital — quarterly reporting, board seats, CAPEX approval thresholds, reserved matters.
- **ESG/sustainability mandates:** Valinor (Helvig) has a strong clean-energy thesis. Future institutional investors will require EU Taxonomy alignment, PUE reporting, renewable certification.
- **Milestone requirements:** Future project finance will be formally milestone-gated (land → permitting → grid → construction → commissioning). Each gate requires professional project controls to demonstrate progress.

### What This Means for Metier
Fossefall's current investors validate the story but don't impose the governance discipline that institutional capital will demand. The multi-billion raise (planned 2026) is the critical inflection point. **Metier can help Fossefall become "bankable"** — providing the project controls, governance frameworks, and execution credibility that infrastructure funds and project finance banks require. This is not just a service opportunity; it is a prerequisite for Fossefall's next funding round.

## 4. Strategic Context

Fossefall targets the AI-HPC segment, positioning Norway as Europe's AI compute platform. Their strategy rests on three pillars: (1) clean energy arbitrage (Nordic hydropower at ~$0.009/kWh vs. continental Europe at >$0.15/kWh), (2) data sovereignty for European enterprises and governments, and (3) vertically integrated AI factory model (power + GPU infrastructure + AI cloud via Seekr and Done.ai partnerships).

The ownership structure implies a deal-making-first approach — the CEO and investor base are finance professionals building an investment platform. Construction capability is being added retroactively (Karl Inge Halleraker hired Jan 2026). The timeline is driven by the AI market window and Seekr's capacity reservation, not by readiness.

- Anchor customer with contracted revenue (Seekr 36-month reservation)
- Operations partner (CBRE Data Centre Solutions for Fyresdal)
- One senior execution hire (Karl Inge Halleraker, ex-Nscale)

### What they're building (active hiring)

- Legal Counsel (project development, commercial agreements)

### What's missing (capability gaps)
- **Project management & controls** — No PMs, cost controllers, planners, or schedulers
- **Procurement & contracts** — No procurement manager, contract manager, or supply chain function
- **Construction management** — One VP Construction cannot deliver 11+ sites; no site managers or field engineers
- **Technical engineering** — No electrical, mechanical, or civil engineers visible
- **Operations staff** — No DC operations roles despite acquiring Fyresdal
- **Portfolio governance** — No governance framework for SPV portfolio; no investor reporting infrastructure
- **ESG/compliance** — No sustainability roles despite clean-energy positioning

## 7. Recommended Pitch

**Lead with: Owner's Engineer / PMC (Service 4) + Project Execution & PM (Service 2)**

**Why:** Fossefall is a financial-DNA company entering physical infrastructure delivery. They have the vision, capital network, and commercial partnerships — but almost no execution capability. The CEO speaks the language of investment returns and deal structure, not construction delivery. Karl Inge Halleraker (VP Construction) is the internal champion — he understands the gap viscerally, having come from Nscale where he had 354 colleagues.

**Strongest opening angle:** Frame Metier as the bridge between Fossefall's investment thesis and physical delivery. Metier makes Fossefall "bankable" — providing the execution credibility that institutional investors and project finance banks require before committing capital. This is not a cost; it is a prerequisite for their next funding round.

**Secondary pitch:** Portfolio Governance (Service 5) — position this as the framework that allows Fossefall to scale from 1 SPV to 11+ without losing control, and that gives institutional investors the reporting visibility they will demand.

## 9. Questions to Ask

1. "What does your financial close timeline look like for Harpefoss and Rollag? Are you in discussions with institutional investors or project finance banks yet?"

2. "How are you structuring project delivery for Fyresdal — are you working with CBRE for both operations and construction, or are you looking for construction/PM support separately?"

3. "With 11+ sites in the pipeline, how are you prioritizing which to develop first? What's driving the sequencing — power availability, permitting readiness, or customer demand?"

4. "What governance and reporting are your current investors (Seekr, Rustad, Helvig) expecting? Are you anticipating more formal requirements from institutional investors?"

5. "Karl Inge — what's your biggest concern about the transition from deal-making to construction delivery? Where do you see the most immediate capability gap?"

6. "Who owns Ølv Holding AS? We want to understand the ultimate control structure as we think about how to work together."

7. "How is the local opposition at Harpefoss and Rollag evolving? Has the March 11 public meeting in Sør-Fron changed anything?"

8. "Are you planning to build an internal PM team, or do you intend to use external partners for project delivery? What's the long-term organizational model?"

## 10. Risks & Objections

- **"We're building our own team."** Possible — the People & Talent Specialist hire signals scaling intent. Counter: "Building takes 12-18 months; your Seekr deadline is 9 months away. We bridge the gap while you hire."

- **"We already have CBRE."** CBRE is an operations partner, not a construction PM firm. Different capability. Clarify the scope of CBRE's engagement.

- **"We're too early-stage to afford Metier."** Counter: "You can't afford not to. Your next funding round depends on demonstrating execution credibility. Professional PM pays for itself in investor confidence."

- **"Kolos comparison is unfair."** It might be — Fossefall has real assets (Fyresdal), real revenue (Seekr), and a real execution hire (Halleraker). But the ambition-to-resource gap echoes the pattern. Frame constructively: "We want to help you prove the doubters wrong."

- **Karl Inge Halleraker may want to build internally** rather than bring in external consultants. He's the gatekeeper. Approach him as a peer, not as a replacement.
