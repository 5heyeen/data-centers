# Prompt 19: applicability-criteria-general-purpose-project-management

> **Prompt:** What criteria should be used to assess whether a general-purpose project management service is genuinely applicable to the data center sector specifically?

---

## Criteria for Assessing DC Applicability of General-Purpose PM Services

**1. Technical complexity threshold**
Does the firm's PM methodology accommodate systems-level interdependencies? DC construction involves critical MEP sequences highly sensitive to design changes. If the firm's approach relies on the client's technical leads to flag interdependencies, it adds limited value and risks compounding delays.

**2. Regulatory and permitting environment**
DCs in Norway sit at the intersection of energy regulation (Statnett grid connection), zoning, environmental permitting, and increasingly national security frameworks. A PM firm must have direct competence in navigating these processes or a reliable specialist ecosystem it can orchestrate. Generic regulatory PM is insufficient.

**3. Client type alignment**
The DC buyer landscape — hyperscalers, colo providers, PE-backed developers, enterprise owner-operators — has radically different procurement and governance cultures. Hyperscalers have internal PMOs and want vendors who can integrate into their processes. PE-backed developers want speed and respond to milestone-gated deliverables. Applicability depends on which client type the firm is targeting.

**4. Procurement norms**
DC procurement involves long-lead equipment categories (transformers, generators, cooling systems) with 40–80 week lead times. A PM firm that treats procurement as a downstream activity will create schedule risk. Applicability requires the firm's process to front-load procurement decisions.

**5. Commissioning complexity**
DC commissioning — testing redundancy systems, verifying N+1/2N configurations, running integrated system tests against live loads — is specialised. PM firms must understand commissioning sequencing or have commissioning management capability.

**6. Speed and iteration**
DC projects are built to hyperscaler or colo market windows, not public sector timelines. A PM firm accustomed to multi-year public infrastructure projects may not have the decision velocity or reporting cadence that DC operators expect.
