# Task Breakdown: Metier–Skanska DC Alliance Research

**Scope:** What could a Metier–Skanska partnership/alliance look like for the Nordic data center market? Research Skanska's DC capabilities, strategy, and track record; map the complementarity with Metier's services; identify structural alliance models; assess competitive positioning; and define the most credible joint value proposition for target operators (Fossefall, Bifrost/Vesten Disse, Bulk).

**Output goal:** Internal strategy memo for Metier leadership to decide whether and how to pursue the alliance.

**User priorities:** Alliance structure models, competitive positioning, target client fit. Flag all risks.

---

### Task 01: Skanska DC Track Record and Capabilities
Research what Skanska has actually built, managed, or bid on in the data center space — Nordic (Norway, Sweden, Finland, Denmark) and globally (US, UK). Identify specific projects with scale (MW or € value), delivery model (design-build, EPCM, GC), client, and year. Capture Skanska's stated DC capabilities from corporate comms, press releases, and job ads. Sources: Skanska.com, DCD, Computer Weekly, DC Byte, press releases, LinkedIn company snippets, project databases. Completion: a sourced inventory of Skanska DC projects plus a capability matrix (what they can self-perform vs. subcontract).

### Task 02: Skanska DC Strategy, Structure, and Business Units
Identify how Skanska is organised around DC — which business unit owns DC (Skanska Norway, Sweden, Finland; Skanska USA Building; Skanska Investor Property?), leadership and named DC figures, strategy statements on DC as a market segment, any M&A or partnership moves in DC, and Skanska Investor Property's reported appetite for DC as an investment category. Sources: Skanska annual reports, investor presentations, corporate strategy docs, LinkedIn/press profiles of DC leaders. Completion: a map of Skanska's DC organisation with named decision-makers and a summary of their stated strategy.

### Task 03: Metier Service Portfolio Mapped to Skanska Gaps
Review Metier's DC service catalog (projects/dc-gtm/dc-services/dc_services_metier.md) and the pain-point mapping (projects/dc-gtm/dc-services/dc_services_metier_pain_point.md). Map each Metier service against what Skanska typically self-performs vs. needs a partner for. Identify concrete complementarity: where does Metier fill a gap Skanska cannot? Where do the two overlap (potential friction)? Norconsult's DC experience should also be mapped because Metier is Norconsult-owned and this affects scope. Sources: local Metier files; Skanska capability statements from Task 01–02. Completion: a service-by-service matrix (Skanska self-performs / Skanska needs partner / Metier provides / overlap risk).

### Task 04: Alliance Structure Models — Options and Precedents
Research alliance structure options relevant to Metier–Skanska: (a) EPCM joint offering, (b) preferred-partner / framework agreement, (c) formal JV, (d) referral / commission arrangement, (e) "star alliance" coalition with MEP/CSA contractors. For each, gather precedent examples from DC and adjacent industries (energy, infra), pros/cons, typical commercial terms, governance, and IP/liability implications. Sources: industry reports, law firm publications on construction JVs, DCD coverage of alliances, academic/consulting literature on alliancing. Completion: a structured comparison of 4–5 alliance models with real precedents and assessed suitability for DC.

### Task 05: Competitive Landscape for DC Project Delivery in the Nordics
Profile the direct and adjacent competitors to a Skanska-Metier DC alliance: CTS Nordics (dominant turnkey), NCC (positioning as Nordic DC player), Hent, Bakke, Veidekke, AF Gruppen, international players (Mercury Engineering, Exyte, ISG, Turner & Townsend, Mace). Capture what each offers (GC, EPC, PM-only), recent DC wins/losses in the Nordics, pricing posture, and any known weaknesses. Pay attention to NCC overlap and flag where NCC is competing for the same ground. Sources: DCD, Finansavisen, Byggeindustrien, company websites, press releases, job ads. Completion: a competitor matrix with scored positioning against a Skanska-Metier alliance.

### Task 06: Target Operator Fit — Fossefall, Bifrost/Vesten Disse, Bulk
Research each target operator's current situation, buying patterns, pain points, and likely receptivity to a Skanska-Metier alliance: Fossefall (Bulk's gigawatt build-out, existing contractor relationships, recent RFQ activity), Bifrost/Vesten Disse (ownership, stage, scale, unmet needs), Bulk (legacy relationships, current contractor mix, stated gaps). Use operator-specific folders under projects/operators/ if they exist, plus public sources. For each, identify (a) why a Skanska-Metier alliance specifically solves their pain, (b) the competing alternative they would likely weigh, (c) the most credible entry point. Sources: local operator briefs, DCD, finn.no, proff.no, brreg.no, operator websites. Completion: a one-page fit assessment per target with a prioritised go-to-market sequence.

### Task 07: Risks, Conflicts, and Structural Obstacles
Identify risks that could derail the alliance: (a) Skanska competing directly where Metier has existing relationships, (b) Norconsult being Metier's parent and also a competing consulting/engineering firm in DC, (c) CTS and international EPCs eroding margin, (d) contractual conflicts (e.g. Skanska as main contractor vs. Metier as owner's rep on the same project), (e) cultural and brand-alignment issues, (f) regulatory or anti-competition concerns for a formal JV in a small Nordic market. Sources: news, regulatory filings, construction law commentary, and cross-referencing Tasks 01–06. Completion: a risk register with likelihood/impact and mitigation options.

### Task 08: Joint Value Proposition and Entry Strategy (Synthesis)
Building on Tasks 01–07, articulate the sharpest joint Metier-Skanska value proposition and the most credible market-entry sequence. Answer: what is the one-line pitch? Which alliance structure fits best? Which of the three target operators should be approached first and how (workshop, RFP response, informal discussion)? What "proof points" must the alliance line up before the first meeting (joint case study, named leads, commercial model)? What does the 90-day activation plan look like? Sources: synthesis of all prior tasks plus any strategic framework references. Completion: a decision-ready recommendation with a named first-move target, structure, and activation plan.
