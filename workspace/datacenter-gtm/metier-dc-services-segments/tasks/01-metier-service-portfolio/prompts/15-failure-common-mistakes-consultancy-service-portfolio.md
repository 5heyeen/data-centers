# Prompt 15: failure-common-mistakes-consultancy-service-portfolio

> **Prompt:** What are the most common mistakes when assessing a consultancy's service portfolio from public sources only? What false conclusions do people reach?

---

## Common Mistakes When Assessing a Consultancy from Public Sources Only

**1. Confusing absence of evidence with evidence of absence.** No case studies in data centers does not mean no experience. Case studies require client consent, marketing investment, and a deliberate decision to signal that vertical publicly.

**2. Equating service line breadth with revenue spread.** A firm listing eight service lines may derive 80% of revenue from two of them. The website reflects what the firm wants to sell, not what it actually sells.

**3. Treating headcount as capability signal.** LinkedIn counts and office locations give almost no signal about specialist depth. A 200-person firm may have three people who actually run major capital programmes.

**4. Mistaking marketing language for methodology.** "Integrated project management," "value-driven delivery," "agile programme governance" appear on nearly every consultancy website. They reveal nothing about actual tools, frameworks, or rigor.

**5. Over-weighting named clients.** The clients a firm can name publicly are often not its most important clients. Anchor relationships — longer, larger, more complex — routinely remain unnamed due to NDAs.

**6. Assuming the website is current.** Consultancy websites are often 2–4 years behind actual capability evolution. A firm that pivoted into infrastructure after an acquisition or hired a new practice lead rarely updates its website promptly.

**The corrective:** Weight reference calls, CVs of the actual proposed team, and financial filings far more heavily than website content when assessing a professional services firm.
